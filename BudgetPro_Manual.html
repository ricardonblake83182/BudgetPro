1. Opening the App
No account, no install, no internet required after first load
1
Find the file
Locate BudgetPro.html — it was shared with you via Google Drive, email, or a web link. Download or bookmark it.
2
Open it in your browser
Double-click the file, or if using a shared link, just tap it. It works in Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge on phone and computer.
3
Bookmark it for easy return
Press Ctrl+D (Windows) or Cmd+D (Mac) to bookmark it. On phone, tap the share icon → "Add to Home Screen" for app-like access.

2. Setting Up Your Budget
Define what you plan to earn and spend each month

Tap the Budget tab. You will see six color-coded categories. Each one has line items with a Budget (what you plan) and an Actual (what you really spent).

🟢 Income
🔵 Savings
🟡 Bills
🟠 Expenses
🔴 Debt
🟣 Investments
1
Clear demo data first
Tap the 🗑️ trash icon next to each demo line item (like "Paycheck 1", "Rent", etc.) to remove the sample data.
2
Add your income sources
Under Income → tap + Add → enter the name (e.g. "Main Job"), budget amount, and actual amount. Add one line per income source.
3
Add all other categories
Repeat for Savings, Bills, Expenses, Debt, and Investments. Tip: enter your budgeted amount first — the actual will update automatically after you sync your bank statement.
4
Edit actuals anytime
The Actual field next to each item is a text box you can type directly into. Changes save automatically.
💡
Important naming tip: When you later import transactions from your bank, the app matches them by subcategory name. So name your budget line items to match your bank's transaction descriptions as closely as possible.

For example: name your bill "T-Mobile" instead of "Phone Bill" so the app can automatically mark it paid when it sees a T-Mobile charge in your statement.

3. Debt Payoff Planner
Get a personalized month-by-month plan to become debt-free faster

The Debt Payoff Planner is found at the bottom of the Budget tab. It calculates exactly how much to pay on each debt every month to pay everything off as fast as possible — and shows you how much interest you'll save compared to just making minimum payments.

1
Go to Budget tab → scroll to the bottom
You'll see the 💳 Debt Payoff Planner section below your budget categories. Tap + Add Debt to begin.
2
Add each debt you're paying off
For each credit card, loan, or balance, enter:
• Debt Name — e.g. "Chase Visa" or "Car Loan"
• Current Balance — how much you still owe
• APR (Interest Rate %) — found on your monthly statement or card website
• Minimum Monthly Payment — the minimum required payment
3
Enter your extra monthly payment
In the "Extra $ toward debt each month" field, enter any amount above your minimums you can afford to put toward debt. Even $50–$100 extra per month makes a significant difference.
4
Choose your strategy
❄️ Snowball — Pay off the smallest balance first. You get quick wins as debts disappear, which keeps you motivated. Great if you need encouragement to stay on track.

🌊 Avalanche — Pay off the highest interest rate first. This saves the most money overall and gets you debt-free the fastest mathematically. Best if you're disciplined and want to minimize total interest paid.
5
Read your plan
The planner shows a table with every debt, the suggested payment for this month, and when each debt will be paid off. The FOCUS debt is the one to attack with your extra payment this month.
📊 How to read the summary cards at the bottom:
• Total Debt — combined balance across all your debts
• Total Minimums/mo — total you must pay just to stay current
• Debt-Free In — estimated months until all debt is eliminated with your plan
• Debt-Free Date — the actual calendar month you'll be debt-free
• Interest Saved — how much less interest you'll pay vs. minimums only
• Time Saved — how many months earlier you'll be debt-free vs. minimums only
💡
Cascade effect: When you fully pay off one debt, the app automatically redirects that minimum payment toward the next debt in line. This is called "cascading" and is what makes the payoff accelerate over time — your payment stays the same but more of it goes toward principal each month.
⭐
Where to find your APR: Look at your monthly statement, log in to your card/loan website, or call the number on the back of your card. It's listed as "APR," "Interest Rate," or "Purchase APR." Credit cards typically range from 18%–29%; car loans from 4%–12%.

Track all income sources with automatic next-date calculation
1
Tap "Add Paycheck"

5. Bills Calendar Tab
See every bill visually on the calendar and track what's paid
1
Add your recurring bills
Tap + Add Bill → enter the bill name, amount, and the day of the month it's due (e.g. Rent is due on the 1st → enter "1").
2
Mark bills as paid
Tap the circle icon next to any bill to toggle it between paid ✅ and unpaid ⭕. You can also tap the bill directly on the calendar.

7. Importing Your Bank or Credit Card Statement
Turn weeks of manual entry into a 2-minute task

Step A — Download your CSV from your bank

Bank / CardWhere to find CSV download
Chase (bank or credit card)Log in → Accounts → select account → Activity → Download → choose CSV → select date range → Download
Bank of AmericaLog in → Accounts → Transactions → Download → Microsoft Excel format (CSV)
Wells FargoLog in → Accounts → Activity → Export → CSV
DiscoverLog in → Statements & Activity → Download → CSV
American ExpressLog in → Statements → Download Transactions → CSV format
Capital OneLog in → Account → Download Transactions → CSV

Step B — Import into BudgetPro

1
Go to the Transactions tab
Tap 📥 Import CSV button (next to "+ Add").
2
Upload your file
Drag your downloaded CSV file onto the drop zone, or tap Choose File to browse. The app detects your bank automatically.
3
Check the column mapping
The app pre-fills the Date, Description, and Amount columns based on your bank's format. Verify they look correct in the preview table — you should see real transaction dates and amounts.
4
Set your default category
Choose a category like Expenses for most bank accounts. Income deposits are detected automatically. Credit card charges default to whatever you choose here.
5
Tap "Import X Transactions"
All transactions are added instantly. The app immediately runs an auto-sync to update your budget, bills, and paychecks.
💳
Credit cards work the same way. Download the CSV from your credit card's website and follow the same steps. Charges import as Expenses. Payments import as Income (since they reduce what you owe).
🔒
Your data is 100% private. The CSV file is read entirely inside your browser — nothing is uploaded to any server or sent over the internet.

8. The Sync Feature
One tap updates the entire app from your transaction data

Sync runs automatically after every import. You can also run it anytime manually by tapping 🔄 Sync Now on the Dashboard.

What sync does automatically:

✓
Updates Budget actuals
Sums all transactions by category and subcategory and fills in the Actual column on the Budget tab. For example: if you have 3 Grocery transactions totaling $312, the Groceries budget line actual becomes $312.
✓
Marks bills as paid
Looks for transactions whose merchant name matches a bill name (e.g. "T-Mobile" matches your Cellphone bill). Any match is automatically marked ✅ Paid on the Bills tab.
✓
Advances paycheck dates
When an income deposit matches a paycheck source, the Next Pay Date on the Paychecks tab advances by your pay frequency automatically.
✓
Refreshes the Dashboard
All charts and KPI cards (Total Income, Total Outflow, Left to Spend, Left to Budget) instantly reflect your real numbers.
💡
When to sync manually: If you add transactions by hand (not via import), tap 🔄 Sync Now on the Dashboard to push those updates through to your Budget actuals and Bills status.
⚡
Best practice: Import your bank statement once a week or at the end of each month. Sync runs automatically each time, keeping everything current with minimal effort.

11. Frequently Asked Questions
Common questions from beta testers
💬 Which strategy should I choose — Snowball or Avalanche?
If your debts are similar in balance, choose Avalanche — it saves the most money. If you have one very small debt you can knock out quickly, start with Snowball for the motivational boost, then switch to Avalanche. You can change the strategy anytime and the planner recalculates instantly.
💬 The debt planner says I'll be debt-free in X months — is that accurate?
It's a close estimate based on your current balance, APR, and payment amount, assuming you make the suggested payment every month without missing one. Real-world results may vary slightly due to billing cycles and rounding, but the planner gives you a solid target to aim for.
The sync matches transactions to budget line items using the Subcategory field. If a transaction's subcategory is blank or doesn't exactly match a budget item name, it won't update that line. Fix it by editing the transaction's subcategory to match the budget item name, then sync again.
💬 A bill wasn't automatically marked paid — why?
The sync looks for a transaction merchant name that contains or matches your bill name. Try renaming your bill to match exactly how your bank lists the payee — for example, change "Phone Bill" to "T-Mobile" if that's how it appears in your bank statement.
💬 Can I import multiple bank statements at once?
Yes — import them one at a time. Each import adds to your existing transactions. Duplicate transactions won't be removed automatically, so avoid importing the same date range twice.
💬 Can my husband and I share the same data?
Not in real-time — each person's browser stores their own data. The recommended approach is for one person to be the primary manager, export CSV monthly, and share those files with the other person for review.
💬 I accidentally deleted something — can I undo it?
Not directly. This is why it's important to export your data regularly. If you export before making big changes, you'll always have a backup to reference.
💬 How do I start a new month fresh?
You don't need to reset anything. The app is designed to accumulate data over time. For bills, you'll need to uncheck paid bills at the start of each month. Budget actuals will update automatically when you import and sync your next bank statement.
💬 How do I leave feedback about the app?
Tap the green "Leave Feedback →" banner at the very top of the app. Your input helps improve the app before it's released publicly — every suggestion counts!
